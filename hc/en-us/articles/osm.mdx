<h1 id="01K1AZH4YVQYB23QQB7SKH6AAW">Option 1: Create a Flow Using a Flow Template</h1>
<p>
  Flow templates provide you with inspiration for crafting your flows based on
  your goals - onboarding, incentivization, and engagement.You can create single-channel
  or multiple-channel flows for sending reminders for abandoned carts, reach out
  to customers who are a churn risk, and improve app/website engagement. The various
  Flow templates are discussed below :
</p>
<h2 id="h_01HYA1V2K3CNAPTPWTZPYDCB1W">Template Types</h2>
<p>There are two basic template types</p>
<ul>
  <li>
    <strong> Adoption</strong>: Adoption templates can be used for onboarding
    new customers and are available for a single channel and multiple channels.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Engagement</strong>: Engagement templates are available for single,
    multiple, or specific channels such as the web and app. They can be used
    for improving app or website engagement, engaging with customers who have
    looked up certain offerings or added certain products to their carts and
    abandoned them, and reaching out to customers who are a churn risk.
  </li>
</ul>

<h2 id="h_01HZRZFMZQRFK781WJKZY2W7QG">Use a Template</h2>
<ol>
  <li>
    Select the flow template and click <strong>Use template</strong>.
  </li>
  <li>
    Add entry conditions, define the target audience, and conversion goal (if
    any) for the flows.
  </li>
  <li>
    Review the campaigns in the template and modify them as required.
  </li>
  <li>
    Add new stages or paths if necessary, as per the business use case.
  </li>
  <li>
    After the structure of your flow is ready, you can click
    <strong>Save</strong> to save it as a draft. To publish the flow, your next
    action depends on your account's settings:
    <ul>
      <li style="list-style-type: none;">
        <ul>
          <li>
            If the Campaign Approval workflow is disabled, you can publish
            the flow directly.
          </li>
          <li>
            If the Campaign Approval workflow is enabled, you have the
            permission to create, manage, and publish campaigns, you
            can publish the flow directly .
          </li>
          <li>
            If the Campaign Approval workflow is enabled and you only
            have the <strong>Create &amp; Manage</strong> permission,
            you must submit the flow for approval. For more information,
            refer
            <a href="/hc/en-us/articles/26100424636436#h_01K0E4E6XW2QY4X7HVPQMGJA1W">here</a>.

<h3 id="h_01J3QDC93ZXZ20YBCYW8WXDSCE">Global Exit Criteria</h3>
<p>
  Under <strong>Flow Exit</strong>, turn the
  <strong>Exit based on conditions</strong> toggle on to define flow exit conditions.
  When the users fulfill the conditions defined, MoEngage removes them from their
  last known position in the flow regardless of where they are. The exit criteria
  are applicable for all flow types (for example,
  <strong>On event trigger</strong>, <strong>At fixed time</strong>,
  <strong>On flow exit</strong>, and <strong>On business event trigger</strong>).
</p>
<p>
  <img src="/guide-media/01J3YZ8TG5KFJGBNT394PA1CSM" alt="Screenshot 2024-07-29 at 3.35.49 PM.png">
</p>
<p>
  MoEngage evaluates users on these conditions right from the time they enter the
  flow to the time they exit the flow due to reasons such as:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>Passing through an exit stage in the flow path</li>
  <li>Fulfilling the conditions in the exit criteria</li>
  <li>Achieving the conversion goal defined in the flow</li>
</ul>
<p>
  You can define the exit criteria using events triggered by users or user properties
  that you track for them:
</p>
<p>
  <img src="/guide-media/01J3YZAXCQPEHTWDKK892K9496" alt="Screenshot 2024-07-29 at 3.35.49 PM.png">
</p>
<ul>
  <li>
    <strong>User property</strong>: On the <strong>User property</strong> tab,
    you can define the exit criteria using user properties by specifying a value
    for a specific attribute. For example:
    <ul>
      <li>
        Remove users when their membership is Gold (this can be used when
        the flow has a fixed agenda to convert users to Gold Membership).
      </li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>User behavior</strong>: On the <strong>User behavior</strong> tab,
    you can define the exit criteria using the list of events tracked in MoEngage.For
    example:
    <ul>
      <li>
        Remove users when they have purchased any product after entering
        the flow
      </li>
      <li>
        Remove users when they have purchased the same product that they
        added to their cart after entering the flow
        <ul>
          <li>
            You can achieve this and similar use cases by linking the
            primary trigger event and the Exit Condition. For more information,
            see
            <a style="background-color: #ffffff; font-family: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;" href="/hc/en-us/articles/21099281569300">Linked Conditions</a>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
        Remove users when they have purchased the same product that is now
        back in stock.
      </li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>

<h2 id="h_01K0E4E6XW2QY4X7HVPQMGJA1W">The Approval Flow</h2>
<div class="tabs tabs--colored-1">
  <div class="tabs-menu">
    <span class="tabs-link is-active">Creator</span>
    <span class="tabs-link">Reviewer</span>
  </div>
  <div class="tab">
    <p>
      As a creator, to send the flow for approval, perform the following steps:
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        On the flow canvas page, select the <strong>Actions</strong> button
        on the right side.
      </li>
      <li>
        In the <strong>Actions</strong> list, select
        <strong>Send for Approval</strong>.&nbsp;The
        <strong>Send for approval</strong> pop-up window appears.<br>
        The MoEngage identifies any configuration errors in your flow which
        are displayed in the <strong>Error</strong> panel on the right side
        of the canvas.You must resolve all listed errors before you can send
        the flow for approval.<br>
        <img src="/guide-media/01K099HNMN3JQ0XVDZNYSBNPK9" width="634" height="379">
      </li>
      <li>
        You must select the users who would review and approve your flow.
        <ol>
          <li>
            In the <strong>Start typing to filter approvers</strong>
            box, search for the users (Admins or Managers) from the team
            you selected in the
            <a href="/hc/en-us/articles/26100424636436#h_01HWK15KM97Q81E3AGRN9E1XB6">Details and Goals</a>
            step.
          </li>
          <li>
            <span class="selected">Click <strong>+ Add next</strong> to the name of each user you want to review the flow. The selected users appear in the <strong>Selected Approvers</strong> section on the right.</span>
          </li>
        </ol>
      </li>
      <li>
        In the <strong>Add yours comment here (optional)</strong> box, you
        can add comments to provide context, instructions, or notes for your
        approvers.
      </li>
      <li>
        Click <strong>Send for approval</strong>.<br>
        <img src="/guide-media/01K0905DGV1XBAA4WBQRJ7R3C7" width="637" height="346">
      </li>
      <li>
        The selected approvers receive an email notification informing them
        that the flow is awaiting their review. While the flow is in the
        <strong>Under Review</strong> state, you have the following options.<br>
        <ol>
          <li>
            <strong>Edit the flow</strong>: You can edit a flow that
            is <strong>Under Review</strong>. After making the edits,
            you must resend the flow for another review by clicking
            <strong>Update and send for approval</strong>. Then, repeat
            steps 3 through 5.<br>
            <img src="/guide-media/01K0998E0EBRSRNGG2RQ0J46XQ" width="602" height="368">
          </li>
          <li>
            <strong>Follow up with approvers</strong>: To follow-up,
            click <strong>Remind Reviewers</strong>.The approvers receive
            a reminder notification.<br>
            <br>
            <img src="/guide-media/01K099DME3MM1J1NE54GJ4Q2HE" width="595" height="354">
          </li>
        </ol>
      </li>
    </ol>
  </div>
  <div class="tab is-hidden">
    <p>To review the flow, perform the following steps:</p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        Open the email you received from MoEngage and click
        <strong>Click to</strong> <strong>Review Flow</strong>.<br>
        <img src="/guide-media/01K09FZYZHK5B2SD64HMV6PYVD" width="610" height="251"><br>
        OR.<br>
        Navigate to the <strong>All Flows</strong> page. In the
        <strong>Select status</strong> filter select the
        <strong>Under Review </strong>checkbox.This provides the list of
        flows awaiting your approval.
      </li>
      <li>
        Click the flow you want to open.<br>
        <img src="/guide-media/01K09DNRPAFSKRW8VA4W4DHRK6" width="612" height="293">
      </li>
    </ol>
    <p>
      You must review the flow by verifying the configurations of every stage.
      After the review is complete, and if there are no corrections required
      for the flow, perform the following steps:
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li style="list-style-type: none;">
        <ol>
          <li>
            On the flow canvas page, click <strong>Approve and Publish</strong>
            on the right side.<br>
            <img src="/guide-media/01K09DBZZDH1BJN5HP73VGE3QB" width="587" height="281"><br>
            The <strong>Approve &amp; publish</strong> flow pop-up window
            appears.
          </li>
          <li>
            In the <strong>Add yours comment here</strong>
            <strong>(optional)</strong> box, add the comments for the
            creator.
          </li>
          <li>
            Click <strong>Approve &amp; publish</strong>.<br>
            <img src="/guide-media/01K09DFMJ9T97XDYBX7V4364QF" width="592" height="251"><br>
            After you publish, the flow becomes <strong>Active</strong>
            or <strong>Scheduled </strong>based on the flow (for example,
            <strong>On event trigger</strong> or <strong>At fixed time</strong>).
            The creator and the other approvers receive an email notification
            informing them that the flow is published.
          </li>
        </ol>
      </li>
    </ol>

        In the <strong>Add your comments here</strong> box, you must add

        Click <strong>Reject flow</strong>.<br>

        an email notification with your comments.
